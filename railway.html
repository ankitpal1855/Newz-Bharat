PM Modi to flag off first Amrit Bharat train soon, informs Railways minister Ashwini Vaishnaw

Inauguration of the First Amrit Bharat Train

The first Amrit Bharat train, a non-AC train that incorporates some features from the Vande Bharat Express, is set to be launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on December 30. The train will run between Ayodhya and Darbhanga in Mithilanchal, the birthplace of Lord Rama and goddess Sita respectively. The second Amrit Bharat train is likely to run in South India.

Innovative Features and Design of Amrit Bharat Train

The Amrit Bharat train is designed with a locomotive at each end, enabling a 'push-pull' operation for faster acceleration and reducing travel time. It will have a maximum permissible speed of 130 kmph and will have light weight foldable snack table, fully sealed gangways, mobile charger with suitable holder and foldable bottle holder. The train will also have aesthetically pleasing and ergonomically designed seat and berth with improved colour blending and cushioned luggage racks.

Amrit Bharat's Comfort and Amenities

The train will have 22 coaches with 12 second-class 3-tier sleeper cars, 8 general second-class coaches, and two guards compartments. In the guard compartments, there will be space for ladies in one coach and differently-abled passengers in another coach. The train will also have zero discharge FRP modular toilet besides aerosol Based Fire suppression system in toilets and electrical cubicles.

Ayodhya to Darbhanga: The Route Significance

The Amrit Bharat train is expected to be in hues of orange and grey. It is part of the Indian Railways' modernisation efforts under the 'Make in India' initiative. The railways minister Ashwini Vaishnaw visited New Delhi Railway station on December 25 to inspect the newly built train. He said that the Amrit Bharat train is a common man's Vande Bharat Express and that it will provide a comfortable and efficient travel experience for passengers.

Expanding Amrit Bharat: Future Plans for South India

Vaishnaw also said that Prime Minister Narendra Modi will soon flag off six more Vande Bharat trains connecting different destinations including Ayodhya-Delhi, Vaishno Devi-New Delhi, Coimbatore-Bengaluru, Jalna-Mumbai, Amritsar-Delhi and Mangaluru Central-Madgaon. He said that these trains are India's first semi-high-speed trains equipped with world-class passenger amenities.

Railway Modernization: Vande Bharat Express Network

The Vande Bharat Express was launched by Prime Minister Narendra Modi on February 15, 2019 as India's first semi-high-speed train. It runs between New Delhi and Varanasi with stops at several other cities along the way. Currently, there are 35 Vande Bharat trains running across the country.
